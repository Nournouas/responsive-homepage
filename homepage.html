About me

As a dedicated high school chemistry teacher with over 15 years of classroom experience, I am passionate about making science both accessible and inspiring for students of all backgrounds. My teaching philosophy centers on curiosity, rigor, and hands-on experimentation — helping students not only understand complex concepts but also see their real-world applications.

My Work

Chemistry of soap

A simplified module that introduces alkanes, alkenes, and alkynes, including molecular models made from classroom kits.

Water Purification Methods in the Classroom

A hands-on unit where students build small filtration systems and test water samples before and after treatment.

The Chemistry of Color: Dyes and Pigments

A teaching project exploring the structure and bonding behind food coloring, clothing dyes, and natural pigments.

Quantifying Reaction Rates with Everyday Materials

A lab experiment where students measure how temperature and surface area affect reaction rates, using safe reactions like baking soda and vinegar.

Safe Storage and Disposal of Laboratory Chemicals

A classroom project focused on best practices for handling, labeling, and disposing of volatile or toxic chemicals safely, aimed at high school science labs.

Crystal Structures Under Controlled Conditions

A detailed independent research project on the production of extremely pure crystalline compounds using specialized lab techniques.
